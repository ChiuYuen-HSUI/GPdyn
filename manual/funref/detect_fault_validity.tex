\subsection*{detect\_fault\_validity} \label{fun:detect_fault_validity}

\textbf{Syntax}
\\  \texttt{function [index\_validity, index\_validity\_window] =
\\ \tab detect\_fault\_validity(X, input, target, NW, var\_residuals)}


\textbf{Description}
\\ Function, which estimates the degree at which we can trust the results of
 the function DETECT\_FAULT. To measure the quality of the
identified model
 particular region, the GP model prediction variances are used.
\\ Note:
\\ (1) Function is not compatible with the current version of the GPML toolbox.
\\ (2) The simulation methods which propagate the variance are recommended,
\\ \tab  eg. simul00mcmc etc.
\\ {[}1{]} Dj.Juricic, J.Kocijan, Fault detection based on Gaussian process model,
 I.Troch,
 \\ \tab F.Breitenecker (eds.), Proceedings of the 5th Vienna
 Symposium on Mathematical \\ \tab Modelling – MathMod, Wien, 2006
\\
\\ Inputs:
\\ X, input, target.. standard GP model inputs
\\ NW .. window size
\\ var\_residuals .. vector of predicted variances
\\ var\_residuals .. [v(1) .. v(n)], where w(k) = var(eps(k))  predicted
      variance associated \\ \tab with $\hat y(k)$, i.e. GP prediction at
time step k
\\ NW .. size of window, used for calculating the index and deciding about fault
\\ c\_max .. maximal significance level still reflecting in deciding for H0
   (no fault)
\\ Outputs:
\\ index\_validity: validity index for time steps Ik
\\   (Ik=0 $\rightarrow$ not valid, Ik=1 $\rightarrow$ valid)
\\ index\_validity\_window: validity index Ik averaged over window NW

\textbf{See Also}
\\ DETECT\_FAULT, SIMUL00MCMC
